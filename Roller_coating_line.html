Roller coating line


Roller coating line - L: Large size system

Ideal for coating flat furniture doors and panels in any type of material. Suitable for any kind of production requirements. Thanks to their simplicity and flexibility, roller coating machines can be used for all types of application, also on the simplest of materials and using UV coatings.



A roller coating line is a type of manufacturing equipment used in various industries for applying a uniform coating to flat surfaces such as metal sheets, plastic films, and wood panels. The process involves feeding the substrate through a series of rollers that apply the coating material, which can be a liquid, powder, or hot-melt material, onto the surface.

Here are some of the key components and uses of a roller coating line:
  1. Unwind Station: This is where the substrate is unwound from a roll and fed into the coating line.
  2. Pre-Treatment Station: The substrate may go through a pre-treatment station to clean and prepare the surface for coating.
  3. Coating Station: The coating station is where the coating material is applied to the substrate. The material is typically stored in a holding tank and fed to the rollers through a pump or other delivery system.
  4. Drying Station: After the coating is applied, the substrate passes through a drying station to cure or solidify the coating material. The drying process may involve heat, infrared radiation, or other techniques depending on the type of coating material.
  5. Cooling Station: The substrate may also pass through a cooling station to bring the temperature down before winding it up into a roll.

Roller coating lines are versatile and efficient pieces of equipment that can help manufacturers produce high-quality coated products with consistent results.
